Improving Production for Eriks Auto Manufacturer

Upgrading traditional single core-processor for Production line robots

Challenges

The traditional single core Processor system controlling the robots struggled to handle complex algorithms needed for real-time adjustments and precise movements on the manufacturing lines, there by slowing down production and constantly breaking down, leading to an increase of over 30% plant downtime with no justifiable explanation for this trend.

What we Achieved

By Upgrading the Processor to a multi-core with parallel processing architecture, it significantly improved the efficiency of the robots; meaning the robots were able to handle computations fasters through distribution of task amongst multiple cores resulting in production increase,with plant uptime of over 90% and smoother robotics operations.

Overview

Elkris management seeks a fundamental understanding of key concepts to help them adopt a suitable architecture for their robots. A comprehensive explanation was provided covering several areas, including: Various computer parts and architectures Hardware security engineering and principles Operating system processes Additionally, the following topics were discussed in detail: Energy-efficient processors: How they contribute to system performance and sustainability Hardware-based security features: Integration of these features to mitigate cyber threats Alternative materials and techniques: Innovative approaches to overcome the limitations of traditional silicon-based processors Operating system functions: Effective management solutions for algorithms, data analysis, and the efficient storage and retrieval of data, particularly in handling large and growing data sets.

Challenges

The primary challenges that may arise are largely dependent on the effectiveness of computer system management. Key considerations include: Ventilation design: The area where computer units are housed must be well-ventilated and equipped with a dedicated cooling system or climate-controlled room to prevent overheating. Moisture protection: The systems must also be kept away from any exposure to moisture, as this can cause significant damage to the computer components. Proper planning and environmental controls are critical to ensuring the longevity and reliability of the equipment.
