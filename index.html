Early Years (1960s-1980s)

The rise of Virus vs the Protection against Virus

1. First computer virus: Created in 1971 by Bob Thomas as an experiment, the "Creeper" virus replicated itself and displayed the message "I'm the creeper, catch me if you can!"

2. First firewall: Developed in 1988 by Marcus J. Ranum, the "DEC SEAL" firewall was designed to control incoming and outgoing network traffic.

Next

1980s: The Rise of Malware

1. First worm: The "Morris Worm" (1988) infected thousands of computers.

2. Antivirus software: First antivirus programs emerged, such as "Cohen's Virus" (1990).

3. Firewalls: First firewalls developed, including the "DEC SEAL" firewall (1988).

1990s: The Internet Expands

1. World Wide Web: Invented in 1989 by Tim Berners-Lee.

2. Internet security threats: Increased malware, phishing, and hacking attacks.

3. SSL/TLS encryption: Emerged as a standard for secure online transactions.

2000s: Cybersecurity Becomes Mainstream

1. Major malware outbreaks: SQL Slammer (2003), Conficker (2008), and Stuxnet (2010).

Compliance regulations: HIPAA (1996), PCI-DSS (2004), and GDPR (2016).

Cloud computing: Emerged as a new paradigm, introducing new security challenges.
